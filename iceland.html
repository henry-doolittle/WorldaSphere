Iceland: The Land of Fire and Ice

Iceland is home to a magestic landscape, and it is unlike anything I have ever seen before. I am yet to find a place with fresher air and more welcoming people. Although some may see it as a barren, dry landscape, it is actually the most amazing country on our list.

Day Three: For breakfast, enjoy a cup of Skyr, a traditional Icelandic food, which your can get at any supermarket. From 9:40-2:00, enjoy yourself at Thingvellir Park, where you can go snorkelling between the tectonic plates at Silfra Fissure, a surreal experience. You can also stand between the tectonic plates, something very unique to this park. Oxarafoss Waterfall is a man-made waterfall that is worth seeing. Also, Althing is the site of the first parliment in the world, located in Thingvellir, and one of the most unique attractions in Iceland. Next, start the 50 min drive to Strokkur, reaching at 3:00, and watch the epic geysers for 1.5 hours. Eat some food and then head to Gulfoss, a waterfall, and leave at 6:00. Check out Hallgrumskirkja Church, Sun Voyager sculpture, and Harpa Concert Hall and then head to bed.

Day Six: Head to Godafoss, a majestic waterfall. Visit Myvatn, a lake, Namaskard, and then head back to the hotel. Then, walk to the community center and experience the amazing place with epic waterslides and pools, geothermally heated, and the legendary ground trampolines, only seen in Iceland, a place that you should not miss. After a day there, head back to the hotel to relax. Also, I recommend the ice cream shop in Akureri, it was delicious. For lunch, you eat at the small shop, Lasagna and more.
